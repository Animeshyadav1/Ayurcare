Back Pain Overview

Understanding the causes, symptoms, and treatment options for back pain.

Overview of Back Pain

Back pain is one of the most common medical problems in the United States. It can range from a dull, constant ache to a sudden, sharp pain that may shoot down the leg. Sometimes it can come on suddenly – from an accident, a fall, or lifting something heavy, or it can develop slowly because of age-related degenerative changes in the spine. In some cases, inflammatory arthritis disorders or other medical conditions cause back pain.

Treatment varies depending on the cause and symptoms, and often there are several contributing factors. However, there are steps you can take to improve your health and lower your chance of developing chronic or long-lasting back pain.

Understanding the Anatomy of the Back

The spine consists of several key structures that work together to support your body and help you move. Problems with any one of these structures can cause back pain.

  • Cervical spine
  • Thoracic spine
  • Lumbar spine
  • Sacrum and coccyx

Other important parts of the back include:

  • Vertebrae – bones that protect the spinal cord.
  • Spinal cord – bundle of nerves running through the vertebrae.
  • Intervertebral discs – cushion-like pads between vertebrae.
  • Ligaments – tissue bands that hold vertebrae in place.
  • Tendons – connect muscles to bones.
  • Muscles – fibers that support the spine and help movement.

Who Gets Back Pain?

Back pain can affect anyone, and almost everyone will experience it at some point in their life. Several factors increase the risk of back pain:

  • Fitness level: Weak back and stomach muscles are more likely to contribute to back pain.
  • Weight gain: Excess weight puts additional strain on the back.
  • Job-related risk factors: Jobs involving heavy lifting or sitting improperly may lead to back pain.
  • Stress level: Chronic stress, poor sleep, or depression may worsen back pain.
  • Age: Back pain becomes more common with age, particularly after 45.
  • Heredity: Genetics can play a role in some back conditions.

Types of Back Pain

Back pain can be categorized in the following ways:

  • Acute back pain: Lasts for a few days to weeks.
  • Subacute back pain: Can last 4 to 12 weeks.
  • Chronic back pain: Lasts longer than 12 weeks and may occur daily.

Symptoms of Back Pain

Symptoms may vary depending on the type and cause of back pain. Common symptoms include:

  • Increasing pain with lifting or bending.
  • Worsening pain when resting, sitting, or standing.
  • Back pain that comes and goes.
  • Morning stiffness and reduced pain with activity.
  • Pain radiating to the buttocks, legs, or hips.
  • Numbness or weakness in legs or feet.

If the pain does not improve after a few weeks or if you experience additional symptoms, consult a doctor:

  • Numbness and tingling.
  • Severe pain that does not improve with medication.
  • Back pain after an injury.
  • Other symptoms like difficulty urinating, weakness, fever, or unexplained weight loss.

Causes of Back Pain

Back pain can be caused by several factors, including mechanical problems, inflammatory conditions, and other medical issues:

  • Mechanical Problems: Sprains, strains, herniated discs, and degenerative changes in the spine.
  • Inflammatory Conditions: Arthritis and ankylosing spondylitis.
  • Other Medical Conditions: Osteoporosis, fibromyalgia, infections, tumors, and pregnancy-related pain.
